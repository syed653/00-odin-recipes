Mincepie

Description

A mince pie is a traditional British pastry enjoyed especially during the Christmas season. Despite its name, it does not typically contain meat in modern recipes. Instead, it is filled with mincemeat, a sweet mixture of dried fruits, spices, and sometimes nuts, combined with suet or a vegetarian fat. The filling is often flavored with festive spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves, and sometimes a splash of brandy or citrus zest is added for extra richness.

The pie is made using a shortcrust pastry, which is buttery, crumbly, and golden brown after baking. Mince pies are typically small, individual-sized treats, perfect for snacking or serving with a cup of tea or mulled wine. They are often dusted with powdered sugar for a snowy, festive appearance.

Historically, mince pies had a savory origin, containing minced meat, spices, and fruit, a practice dating back to the medieval period. Over time, the recipe evolved into the sweet treat we know today, retaining its name as a nod to its origins.

Ingredients

  • Pastry:
    • 225g (8 oz) plain flour
    • 100g (4 oz) unsalted butter, chilled and diced
    • 2-3 tablespoons cold water
    • Pinch of salt
  • Filling:
    • 300g (10 oz) mincemeat (fruit-based mixture, not meat!)
  • Optional: Powdered sugar for dusting

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F, Gas Mark 6).
  2. Make the pastry:
    • Combine the flour and salt in a bowl.
    • Rub in the butter until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
    • Add cold water a little at a time and mix until the dough comes together.
    • Wrap the dough in cling film and chill for 20 minutes.
  3. Roll out the pastry on a floured surface to about 3mm thick.
  4. Cut out circles to fit your tart tin, and press the pastry into the molds.
  5. Fill each pastry shell with a spoonful of mincemeat.
  6. Cut out smaller circles or decorative shapes for the lids and place them on top.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown.
  8. Cool on a wire rack and dust with powdered sugar before serving.
