Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a beloved Italian dish consisting of multiple layers of wide pasta sheets, savory fillings, and rich sauces. Traditionally, it features layers of meat sauce (like Bolognese), béchamel (a creamy white sauce), and cheese (often mozzarella and Parmesan).

The dish is then baked until golden and bubbly, creating a deliciously comforting and hearty meal. Variations include vegetarian versions with spinach, mushrooms, or other vegetables. The combination of textures and flavors makes lasagna a satisfying and iconic comfort food.

Ingredients

  • Pasta Lasagna noodles: Wide, flat pasta sheets. They can be pre-cooked or no-boil, depending on the recipe.
  • Sauce: Tomato sauce or marinara: Often combined with ground meat or vegetables for a hearty ragu. Béchamel sauce (optional in some recipes): A creamy white sauce made from butter, flour, and milk.
  • Filling Meat: Ground beef, pork, sausage, or a combination for a traditional meat sauce. Vegetarian alternatives: Sautéed vegetables like spinach, mushrooms, zucchini, or eggplant.
  • Cheese Ricotta cheese: Often mixed with egg, herbs, and sometimes Parmesan. Mozzarella cheese: For layering and topping; it melts beautifully. Parmesan cheese: Adds sharp, nutty flavor.
  • Seasonings Herbs and spices: Basil, oregano, garlic, salt, and pepper. Some recipes may include thyme or parsley.

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles if required, then set them aside.
  3. Prepare the sauce: Cook ground meat or vegetables, then mix with tomato sauce and seasonings.
  4. Mix ricotta cheese with egg and herbs.
  5. Assemble the lasagna in a baking dish:
    • Layer 1: Sauce
    • Layer 2: Pasta
    • Layer 3: Ricotta mixture
    • Layer 4: Mozzarella and Parmesan
    • Repeat until the dish is full, ending with cheese on top.
  6. Bake for 30-40 minutes, or until bubbling and golden on top.
  7. Let rest for 10 minutes before serving.
